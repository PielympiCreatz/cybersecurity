KEY CONCEPTS

Cybersecurity focuses on protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attacks. Digital forensics involves the identification, collection, and analysis of digital evidence to investigate cyber incidents. Together, these fields work to prevent, detect, and respond to cyber threats while preserving the integrity of digital information.

COMMON THREATS & INVESTIGATION TECHNIQUES

Cyber threats include malware, phishing, ransomware, insider attacks, and denial-of-service attempts. Digital forensic investigators use techniques such as disk imaging, log analysis, and metadata examination to trace attacks and recover evidence. Maintaining a proper chain of custody is essential to ensure that digital evidence is admissible in legal proceedings.

APPLICATIONS & REAL WORLD IMPACT

Cybersecurity and digital forensics are critical in protecting personal, corporate, and government data. They are applied in incident response, criminal investigations, and legal cases involving cybercrime. Emerging technologies like AI-driven threat detection and cloud forensics are shaping how professionals prevent attacks and analyze digital evidence, ensuring safer digital environments
